Background

From Islamabad to Shenzhen to Toronto

I did not plan to end up as the person a national government regulator calls directly. But that is what happens when you combine technical depth with commercial instinct across three continents.

I grew up in Pakistan, earned my electrical engineering degree in Islamabad, then moved to Beijing for my MSc in Power Systems where I published peer-reviewed research on harmonic analysis between wind farms and HVDC transmission systems. That was my first real taste of working in a fully Mandarin environment, surrounded by colleagues who had never worked with someone like me. I learned to adapt fast.

After my degree I moved to Shenzhen, the heart of China's electronics manufacturing industry. At WSCT, I was doing hands-on RF and EMC testing in the morning and flying to meet government regulators in Laos, Botswana, and Sudan in the afternoon. I learned what it means to walk into a room with a ministry official and earn their trust, not with a pitch deck, but with technical credibility and genuine listening.

The Tecno Mobile moment defined my career. I heard through my industry network that the company had a compliance problem. Nobody had approached them yet. I spent days trying to reach the right person through every channel available. When I finally got the meeting, both parties already knew what needed to be discussed. What followed was 2.5 months of negotiations involving leadership on both sides. We closed one of WSCT's largest contracts. Infinix Mobility followed shortly after, drawn by the Tecno success story.

"I understand compliance from both sides of the table, from inside the lab running the tests, and from inside the boardroom explaining what the results mean for a company's global market access."

At SGS I became the dedicated account manager for Egypt's NTRA, the national telecom regulator. They called me directly for everything including scope changes, compliance questions, errors, and new requirements. I built their entire certification process from the ground up. When I joined, errors happened daily. By the time I left, fewer than 4 per month.

Where I have made an impact

Three roles across two companies, two countries, and three continents, each one building on the last.

SGS — Société Générale de Surveillance
Project Engineer and Egypt Market Account Manager
January 2021 to January 2025 · Shenzhen, China
Presenting at a technical compliance conference, Shenzhen

SGS is one of the world's largest TIC companies with 97,000 employees across 130 countries. Direct competitors include TÜV SÜD, Bureau Veritas, Intertek, and CSA Group. I joined as a Project Engineer and became the single point of contact for Egypt's national telecom regulator, NTRA.

What that meant in practice was that NTRA called me directly. For every scope change, every compliance question, every error, every new standard, I was the person who picked up. I did not escalate and wait. I diagnosed, coordinated with the test teams, communicated the corrective actions, and followed up until it was resolved. 90% of issues closed within 24 hours.

When I joined there was no Egypt market certification framework. I built it from scratch including guidelines, procedures, checklists, and quality controls. Certification errors went from a daily occurrence to fewer than 4 per month. I also reviewed 20 or more technical test reports daily for compliance with CE, FCC, ETSI-EN, VCCI, and AS/NZS standards, and trained both technical and sales teams on the process, reducing onboarding time by 20%.

I also participated in the A2LA external laboratory accreditation audit, one of the most rigorous accreditation processes in the industry.
